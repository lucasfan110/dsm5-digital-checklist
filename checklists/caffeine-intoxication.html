Caffeine Intoxication

  1. A. Recent consumption of caffeine (typically a high dose well in excess of 250 mg).
  2. B. Five (or more) of the following signs or symptoms developing during, or shortly after, caffeine use:
    1. 1. Restlessness.
    2. 2. Nervousness.
    3. 3. Excitement.
    4. 4. Insomnia.
    5. 5. Flushed face.
    6. 6. Diuresis.
    7. 7. Gastrointestinal disturbance.
    8. 8. Muscle twitching.
    9. 9. Rambling flow of thought and speech. 10. Tachycardia or cardiac arrhythmia. 11. Periods of inexhaustibility. 12. Psychomotor agitation.
  3. C. The signs or symptoms in Criterion B cause clinically significant distress or impairment in social, occupational, or other important areas of functioning.
  4. D. The signs or symptoms are not attributable to another medical condition and are not better explained by another mental disorder, including intoxication with another substance.
