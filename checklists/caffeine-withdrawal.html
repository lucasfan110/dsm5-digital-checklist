Caffeine Withdrawal

  1. A. Prolonged daily use of caffeine.
  2. B. Abrupt cessation of or reduction in caffeine use, followed within 24 hours by three (or more) of the following signs or symptoms:
    1. 1. Headache.
    2. 2. Marked fatigue or drowsiness.
    3. 3. Dysphoric mood, depressed mood, or irritability.
    4. 4. Difficulty concentrating.
    5. 5. Flu-like symptoms (nausea, vomiting, or muscle pain/stiffness). 572
  3. C. The signs or symptoms in Criterion B cause clinically significant distress or impairment in social, occupational, or other important areas of functioning.
  4. D. The signs or symptoms are not associated with the physiological effects of another medical condition (e.g., migraine, viral illness) and are not better explained by another mental disorder, including intoxication or withdrawal from another substance.
