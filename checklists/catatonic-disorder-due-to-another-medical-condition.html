Catatonic Disorder Due to Another Medical Condition

  1. A. The clinical picture is dominated by three (or more) of the following symptoms:
    1. 1. Stupor (i.e., no psychomotor activity; not actively relating to environment).
    2. 2. Catalepsy (i.e., passive induction of a posture held against gravity).
    3. 3. Waxy flexibility (i.e., slight, even resistance to positioning by examiner).
    4. 4. Mutism (i.e., no, or very little, verbal response [Note: not applicable if there is an established aphasia]).
    5. 5. Negativism (i.e., opposition or no response to instructions or external stimuli).
    6. 6. Posturing (i.e., spontaneous and active maintenance of a posture against gravity).
    7. 7. Mannerism (i.e., odd, circumstantial caricature of normal actions).
    8. 8. Stereotypy (i.e., repetitive, abnormally frequent, non-goal- directed movements).
    9. 9. Agitation, not influenced by external stimuli. 10. Grimacing. 11. Echolalia (i.e., mimicking another’s speech). 12. Echopraxia (i.e., mimicking another’s movements).
  2. B. There is evidence from the history, physical examination, or laboratory findings that the disturbance is the direct pathophysiological consequence of another medical condition.
  3. C. The disturbance is not better explained by another mental disorder (e.g., a manic episode).
  4. D. The disturbance does not occur exclusively during the course of a delirium. 137
  5. E. The disturbance causes clinically significant distress or impairment in social, occupational, or other important areas of functioning.
  6. Coding note:

    Include the name of the medical condition in the name of the mental disorder (e.g., F06.1 catatonic disorder due to hepatic encephalopathy). The other medical condition should be coded and listed separately immediately before the catatonic disorder due to the medical condition (e.g., K72.90 hepatic encephalopathy; F06.1 catatonic disorder due to hepatic encephalopathy).
