Major or Mild Neurocognitive Disorder Due to Prion Disease

  1. A. The criteria are met for major or mild neurocognitive disorder.
  2. B. There is insidious onset, and rapid progression of impairment is common.
  3. C. There are motor features of prion disease, such as myoclonus or ataxia, or biomarker evidence.
  4. D. The neurocognitive disorder is not attributable to another medical condition and is not better explained by another mental disorder.
  5. Coding note (see coding table on pp. 682–683):

    For major neurocognitive disorder due to prion disease, with behavioral disturbance, code first A81.9 prion disease, followed by F02.81 major neurocognitive disorder due to prion disease, with behavioral disturbance. For major neurocognitive disorder due to prion disease, without behavioral disturbance, code first A81.9 prion disease, followed by F02.80 major neurocognitive disorder due to prion disease, without behavioral disturbance.
