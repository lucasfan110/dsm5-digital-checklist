Tic Disorders

  1. Note:

    A tic is a sudden, rapid, recurrent, nonrhythmic motor

  2. Tourette's Disorder

  3. A. Both multiple motor and one or more vocal tics have been present at some time during the illness, although not necessarily concurrently.
  4. B. The tics may wax and wane in frequency but have persisted for more than 1 year since first tic onset.
  5. C. Onset is before age 18 years.
  6. D. The disturbance is not attributable to the physiological effects of a substance (e.g., cocaine) or another medical condition (e.g., Huntington’s disease, postviral encephalitis). Persistent (Chronic) Motor or Vocal Tic Disorder F95.1

  7. Persistent (Chronic) Motor or Vocal Tic Disorder

  8. A. Single or multiple motor or vocal tics have been present during the illness, but not both motor and vocal.
  9. B. The tics may wax and wane in frequency but have persisted for more than 1 year since first tic onset.
  10. C. Onset is before age 18 years.
  11. D. The disturbance is not attributable to the physiological effects of a substance (e.g., cocaine) or another medical condition (e.g., Huntington’s disease, postviral encephalitis).
  12. E. Criteria have never been met for Tourette’s disorder.
  13. Specify if:

    With motor tics only With vocal tics only Provisional Tic Disorder F95.0

  14. Provisional Tic Disorder

  15. A. Single or multiple motor and/or vocal tics.
  16. B. The tics have been present for less than 1 year since first tic onset.
  17. C. Onset is before age 18 years.
  18. D. The disturbance is not attributable to the physiological effects of a substance (e.g., cocaine) or another medical condition (e.g., Huntington’s disease, postviral encephalitis).
  19. E. Criteria have never been met for Tourette’s disorder or persistent (chronic) motor or vocal tic disorder. Specifiers The “motor tics only” or “vocal tics only” specifier is only required for persistent (chronic) motor or vocal tic disorder.
